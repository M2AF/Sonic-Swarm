### 2. Fix the Missing Album Art

The reason the artwork disappears is likely because when you click an album, you are passing an object that *only* contains the track list or metadata, losing the `artworkUrl` that was present in the search result.

**Step 1: Capture the art in `handlePlayAlbum**`
Look for where you define `handlePlayAlbum` in `App.jsx`. Ensure you are passing the image URL into the state:

```javascript
const handlePlayAlbum = (album) => {
  setCurrentAlbum({
    ...album,
    // Ensure the artwork URL from the iTunes search result is preserved
    artworkUrl: album.artworkUrl600 || album.artworkUrl100 || album.artworkUrl
  });
  setView('album');
};

```

**Step 2: Render it in the detail view**
Find the `div` in your `App.jsx` that renders the album detail view (usually where you map `currentAlbum.tracks`). Add the image tag:

```jsx
{/* Inside your Album Detail View */}
<div className="album-hero">
  <img 
    src={currentAlbum.artworkUrl} 
    alt={currentAlbum.title} 
    className="album-art-large"
  />
  <div className="album-meta">
    <h2>{currentAlbum.title}</h2>
    <p>{currentAlbum.artist}</p>
  </div>
</div>

```

### Why this works:

1. **State Persistence:** By explicitly mapping `artworkUrl` in `handlePlayAlbum`, you ensure that the image path doesn't get lost when the app switches from `search` view to `album` view.
2. **Shared Logic:** By extracting your skip/auto-advance logic into a shared `executeTrackChange` function, you guarantee that manual skips are just as "smart" as auto-advances—they will respect your FLAC/MP3 preferences and attempt to stay within the same torrent file index whenever possible.

**One final tip:** Since you are doing a lot of state manipulation, if the buttons still don't feel "responsive," make sure your `audioRef` update in `handleEnded` (the one I gave you previously with `.load()`) is also inside your `executeTrackChange` function so the browser knows the source has changed.
